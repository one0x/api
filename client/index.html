Benefits of Blockchain
in education

Blockchain technology allows for a total disintermediation and disaggregation of education.

  • The centralised model present day learning is no longer sustainable.
  • Today’s students receive an education in both formal and informal learning settings, not just traditional brick-and-mortar university lecture halls and blockchain enables equal crediting for them all.
View case study

Permanent Education Records

>

Even if the institutions that issued the certificates were to close down, or if the entire system of education collapses (as, for instance, happened in Syria), those certificates are still verifiable against the records stored in a blockchain.

View case study

Gain ownership and control of your learning data

  • Blockchain allows personal data to be just that —personal.
  • Students gain control and ownership of all their education data, including accreditation and portfolios of work. No more running around .
View case study

Analyse your learning and impact

  • "Imagine a scenario where every learning activity is registered on the Blockchain, including informal learning – together with informal feedback,"
  • You can map all assessment results on learning environment across a country and develop country wide analytics. The best lecturers by subject in a can could be easily identified.
View case study

Micro-accreditation

  • Currently, the verification process of resumes or transcripts involves consulting the institution itself, determining the quality of the accreditation the institution claims to have, confirming this issuance with the accrediting body itself, and checking with the governing authority to see if the accrediting body is authorized to operate in its particular capacity.
  • With blockchain, you can do this in a single click by looking at the pedigree of an assessment and visualising its accreditation chain.
View case study

Payment and funding

  • Eliminate barriers such as restricted access to bank accounts or credit cards depending on country of origin.
  • Create self-regulated scholarships to fund education of the underprivileged or encourage higher learning for the passionate learners.
